# Use Cases

BlackoutPlug is useful when a person needs to know whether a physical location has power.

## UC-01: Home monitoring while away

**User:** homeowner or renter  
**Place:** apartment or house  
**Device:** Shelly Plug connected at home  
**Hosting:** BlackoutPlug stack on VPS

### Flow

1. User creates a private site in Telegram.
2. User configures the Shelly Plug MQTT settings with values generated by BlackoutPlug.
3. The plug publishes status to the VPS MQTT broker.
4. User receives Telegram notifications when power goes off or comes back.

### Value

The user knows what happened at home without depending on someone physically checking.

## UC-02: Parents / relatives

**User:** family member  
**Place:** parents’ or relatives’ home

### Flow

1. One person installs the Shelly Plug.
2. The site owner shares a code or QR invite card with family members.
3. Family members subscribe in Telegram.
4. Everyone receives outage/restore notifications.

### Value

The family has a shared signal without a noisy group chat interrogation every time the lights blink.

## UC-03: Neighbor or building group

**User:** local community, apartment building, nearby houses

### Flow

1. One monitored site acts as a local signal.
2. Other people join with a share code, Telegram invite link, or QR card shown in person.
3. Subscribers receive the same site status and history.

### Value

A group gets an early practical signal about local power availability without requiring everyone to know each other first.

## UC-04: Café, shop, or small office

**User:** owner, manager, staff, clients  
**Place:** small business location

### Flow

1. Staff install and configure a Shelly Plug.
2. BlackoutPlug monitors the site from the VPS.
3. Owner, staff and clients share the site with a QR invite card or Telegram link.
4. Owner, staff and clients receive Telegram notifications.
5. They check history after incidents.

### Value

The business can react faster when power affects routers, payment terminals, fridges, cameras, or scheduled work.
Clients can check whether power and public Wi-Fi are currently available at the café.
